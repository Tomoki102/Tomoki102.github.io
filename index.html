Research Motivation

    Human progress advances with storage—first grain and water, then data, now energy. My motivation is to advance our capacity to store energy through battery research. This is more than sustainability; better storage can expand what people and societies can do. As the energy gap between people and their devices closes, the fusion of human ability and machine function accelerates. At the societal level, as systems for renewable conversion and battery storage permeate infrastructure, previously overlooked ambient energy becomes a usable resource for all. To achieve this future, rechargeable batteries must couple high energy density with chemistries based on earth-abundant elements, rigorous safety, and strong biocompatibility for on-body use. These requirements define my research focus: the design and mechanistic understanding of systems that store more energy, minimize self-discharge and degradation, and integrate safely into daily life.
